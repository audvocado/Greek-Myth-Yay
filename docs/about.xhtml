Project Details

Methodology

Our research utilizes XML markup and computational text analysis to track how specific female characters are described across different Greek genres. By tagging instances of dialogue, epithets, and emotional markers, we can visualize the contrast between a character's agency and the narrator's bias.

Research Questions

  • How does motherhood influence a character's "power level" in the eyes of a male narrator?
  • Is there a linguistic pattern that links female intelligence to witchcraft or deception?
  • Do female characters speak more or less depending on their social status in the text?

Project Goals

Ultimately, this project seeks to challenge traditional readings of Greek literature by highlighting the complex, often contradictory ways power was granted to—and stripped from—women in the ancient world.
