230Of all creatures that have life and reason
231we women are the sorriest lot:
232first we must at a great expenditure of money
233buy a husband and even take on a master
234over our body: this evil is more galling than the first.
235Here is the most challenging contest, whether we will get a bad man
236or a good one. Besides, divorce is unsavory
237for a woman and it is not possible to say no to one's husband.
238And when she comes into new customs and rules
239a woman must be a prophet of what she could never learn at home:
240how best to deal with her marriage partner;
241and if we get it worked out well and a husband shares
242our life with us, and he bears the yoke without violence,
243life is to be envied. Otherwise we are better off dead.
244But the man, when he is bored with things at home
245he can go out to ease the weariness of his heart.
246But we have just one person to look to.
247They say that we live a life free of danger
248at home while they face battle with the spear.
249How wrong they are. I would rather stand three times
250in the line of battle than once bear a child.
251But the same story does not apply to you and me.
252You have this city and your father's home,
253enjoyment of life, and the companionship of friends,
254but, alone and without a city, I am abused
255by my husband, carried off as plunder from a foreign land,
256I have no mother, no brother, no relative
257to offer me a safe haven from this disaster.
